## Portfolio Website User Stories

These user stories describe the desired functionality and experience from the perspective of a visitor to the portfolio website.

***

### US1 – Homepage

**As a visitor, I want to see:**

*   A hero section featuring an image, name, and introductory text, so I immediately know who the portfolio belongs to.
*   Clear navigation links to projects, skills, vision, and contact sections, so I can navigate the site easily.

***

### US2 – Projects

**As a visitor, I want to:**

*   View projects presented in cards that include an image, name, and brief introduction, so I can quickly browse and assess them.
*   Click on a project card to view full details, including a gallery of images/media and supporting documentation, so I can understand the project thoroughly.

***

### US3 – Skills & Resume

**As a visitor, I want to see:**

*   A dedicated section detailing professional skills, including the name of the skill, associated school/institution (if applicable), and the period of experience, so I understand your expertise and background.
*   An option to download your resume in a standard format (e.g., PDF), so I can easily save or print your qualifications.
*   Technical skills logically grouped by category (e.g., Programming Languages, Frameworks, Tools) for improved readability and comprehension.
*   Certificates displayed with their respective images and names, providing visual proof of completed qualifications.

***

### US4 – Contact

**As a visitor, I want to:**

*   Submit a contact form requiring my name, email address, subject, and message, so I have a direct and simple way to reach you.

***

### US5 – Vision

**As a visitor, I want to see:**

*   A "Vision" section featuring a title, description, a scrollable list of guiding principles, and stated long-term goals, so I can understand your professional ambitions and philosophy.

***

### US6 – 404 Page (Error Handling)

**As a visitor, I want to see:**

*   A friendly, informative 404 error page with navigation options, so I know I have reached a broken or non-existent link and can easily return to the main site.
